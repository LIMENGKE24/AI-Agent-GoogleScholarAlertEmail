## Selected Papers and Summaries

### 1. The Pursuit for High Power Density in Silicon‐Based ASSBs
- **Summary:** This paper investigates the challenges and potential of using silicon anodes in all-solid-state batteries (ASSBs) to achieve high power density. It highlights how solid electrolytes can suppress parasitic reactions and manage silicon's volume expansion, but also identifies interfacial and kinetic limitations.
- **Key Insights:**
    - **Methods:** Review of recent experimental and modeling studies on Si-based ASSBs.
    - **Findings:** Solid electrolytes improve safety and energy density, but interfacial resistance and slow ion transport in Si limit power performance.
    - **Limitations:** Focuses primarily on Si anodes; may not generalize to other chemistries.
    - **Potential Applications:** High-energy, high-power electric vehicles and portable electronics.
- **Direct Link:** [https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/aenm.202505061](https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/aenm.202505061)

### 2. Phase Diagrams Enable Solid‐State Battery Design
- **Summary:** This perspective article argues that thermodynamic driving forces, specifically chemical potential mismatches at interfaces, are fundamental to understanding and predicting degradation in solid-state batteries. It uses phase diagrams as a tool to design more stable interfaces.
- **Key Insights:**
    - **Methods:** Thermodynamic analysis and phase diagram calculations for the Li|Li3PS4|S system.
    - **Findings:** Interfacial reactions are driven by chemical potential differences, not just kinetics. Phase diagrams can predict stable interface configurations.
    - **Limitations:** The approach is conceptual and requires extensive thermodynamic data for new material systems.
    - **Potential Applications:** Rational design of stable solid electrolyte/cathode and solid electrolyte/anode interfaces.
- **Direct Link:** [https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdfdirect/10.1002/admi.202500800](https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdfdirect/10.1002/admi.202500800)

### 3. Halide solid electrolytes: composition tuning, structural design, and performance optimization
- **Summary:** This review provides a comprehensive overview of halide solid electrolytes for ASSBs, covering their material chemistry, crystal structures, synthesis methods, and the relationship between composition, structure, and performance.
- **Key Insights:**
    - **Methods:** A systematic review of literature on halide electrolytes.
    - **Findings:** Halides offer high ionic conductivity and good stability with high-voltage cathodes. Halogen type and mixing significantly impact ion transport and stability.
    - **Limitations:** Many halides are unstable against lithium metal, limiting their use with Li-metal anodes.
    - **Potential Applications:** Enabling high-voltage cathodes in next-generation ASSBs.
- **Direct Link:** [https://pubs.rsc.org/en/content/articlehtml/2025/ta/d5ta07277h](https://pubs.rsc.org/en/content/articlehtml/2025/ta/d5ta07277h)

### 4. Review on Cathode‐Electrolyte Interphase for Stabilizing Interfaces in Solid‐State Lithium Batteries
- **Summary:** This review examines the complex and dynamic nature of the cathode-electrolyte interphase (CEI) in solid-state lithium batteries. It discusses the formation mechanisms, evolution during cycling, and strategies for stabilizing the CEI.
- **Key Insights:**
    - **Methods:** Review of experimental and theoretical studies on CEI formation and evolution.
    - **Findings:** The CEI is continuously reconfigured by structural degradation of the cathode and decomposition of the solid electrolyte. Stabilizing the CEI is critical for long-term performance.
    - **Limitations:** The CEI is highly complex and system-specific, making universal stabilization strategies challenging.
    - **Potential Applications:** Improving the cycle life and performance of high-energy ASSBs.
- **Direct Link:** [https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdf/10.1002/advs.202517032](https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdf/10.1002/advs.202517032)

### 5. Effective Li-Ion Transport Quantification in Composite Cathodes for All-Solid-State Batteries via Multiscale Modeling and Experiments
- **Summary:** This work quantifies the effective lithium-ion transport in composite cathodes for ASSBs by linking microscale tortuosity to macroscale cell performance through a combined simulation and experimental approach.
- **Key Insights:**
    - **Methods:** Multiscale modeling (FIB-SEM tomography, finite element modeling) validated by electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) and galvanostatic cycling.
    - **Findings:** The tortuosity factor in composite cathodes is a key parameter affecting rate performance and differs significantly from liquid electrolyte systems.
    - **Limitations:** The model is complex and requires detailed microstructural data.
    - **Potential Applications:** Optimizing cathode microstructure for high-power ASSBs.
- **Direct Link:** [https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/acs.chemmater.5c02212](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/acs.chemmater.5c02212)

### 6. A high-entropy mixed ionic and electronic conductor for accelerating the cathode dynamics in all solid–state lithium metal batteries
- **Summary:** This paper introduces a high-entropy mixed ionic and electronic conductor (HE-O-MIEC) to improve cathode dynamics in ASSLMBs. The material simplifies the composite cathode structure by combining ionic and electronic conductivity in a single phase.
- **Key Insights:**
    - **Methods:** Synthesis and characterization of Li1/6-x(LaPrNdSrBa)1/6CoO3-δ, including electrochemical testing.
    - **Findings:** The HE-O-MIEC exhibits high electronic conductivity and good ionic conductivity, leading to improved rate capability and reduced interfacial resistance.
    - **Limitations:** The synthesis of high-entropy materials can be complex and may have scalability issues.
    - **Potential Applications:** Simplifying cathode design and improving performance of high-energy ASSLMBs.
- **Direct Link:** [https://www.science.org/doi/full/10.1126/sciadv.adw4710](https://www.science.org/doi/full/10.1126/sciadv.adw4710)

### 7. Revisiting Carbon Materials for Practical Sulfide‐Based Solid‐State Lithium Batteries
- **Summary:** This review re-examines the role of carbon materials in sulfide-based solid-state lithium batteries. It discusses the challenges and opportunities associated with using carbon as an electronic conductor in the rigid solid-solid interfaces of these batteries.
- **Key Insights:**
    - **Methods:** A critical review of the literature on carbon in sulfide-based SSLBs.
    - **Findings:** Carbon materials are essential but can cause mechanical instability and reaction kinetics issues. Understanding their behavior is key to practical SSLBs.
    - **Limitations:** The behavior of carbon is highly dependent on the specific system and processing conditions.
    - **Potential Applications:** Designing more stable and high-performance composite cathodes for sulfide-based SSLBs.
- **Direct Link:** [https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/adfm.202523724](https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/adfm.202523724)

### 8. Lithium-Compatible Halide Superionic Conductors
- **Summary:** This work reports on the development of lithium-compatible halide superionic conductors that overcome the reduction instability of typical halides, enabling their use with lithium metal anodes.
- **Key Insights:**
    - **Methods:** Materials synthesis, structural characterization, and electrochemical testing of new halide compositions.
    - **Findings:** Specific halide compositions show high ionic conductivity and improved stability against lithium metal.
    - **Limitations:** The long-term stability and scalability of these new materials need further investigation.
    - **Potential Applications:** Enabling high-energy-density ASSBs with lithium metal anodes.
- **Direct Link:** [https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/acs.jpclett.5c02453](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/acs.jpclett.5c02453)

### 9. Elastic softening and defect-mediated diffusion in superionic Li 2 O revealed by molecular dynamics
- **Summary:** This study uses machine learning potentials in molecular dynamics simulations to investigate the superionic transition in Li2O, revealing the mechanisms of elastic softening and defect-mediated diffusion.
- **Key Insights:**
    - **Methods:** Machine learning potentials for molecular dynamics simulations of Li2O.
    - **Findings:** The superionic transition is driven by elastic softening and defect-mediated diffusion, providing a new understanding of ion transport in this material.
    - **Limitations:** The simulations are computationally intensive and the findings need experimental validation.
    - **Potential Applications:** Designing new solid electrolytes with enhanced ionic conductivity.
- **Direct Link:** [https://link.aps.org/pdf/10.1103/4z6n-zfdr](https://link.aps.org/pdf/10.1103/4z6n-zfdr)

### 10. The Latent Culprit of Anode‐Free Lithium Metal Batteries Degradation
- **Summary:** This paper identifies the complex interfacial dynamics and evolution unique to anode-free lithium metal batteries (AFLMBs) as the primary cause of rapid capacity fade, driven by lithium loss and electrolyte depletion.
- **Key Insights:**
    - **Methods:** Electrochemical testing, microscopy, and spectroscopy to study degradation in AFLMBs.
    - **Findings:** The formation of inactive lithium and depletion of the electrolyte at the current collector/electrolyte interface are the main degradation mechanisms.
    - **Limitations:** The study focuses on a specific system, and the findings may not be generalizable to all AFLMBs.
    - **Potential Applications:** Improving the cycle life and energy density of AFLMBs for next-generation energy storage.
- **Direct Link:** [https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/adfm.202524486](https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/adfm.202524486)

## Five New Research Ideas

### 1. Machine Learning-Guided Discovery of Multi-Anion Halide Solid Electrolytes
- **Rationale:** Halide electrolytes offer high conductivity and cathode stability but often suffer from reduction instability. Multi-anion strategies (e.g., mixing Cl, Br, I) could tune the electronic structure for better stability, but the compositional space is vast.
- **Feasibility:** High. DFT calculations can generate training data for ML models to predict stability and conductivity. Experimental validation is straightforward.
- **Potential Impact:** Could unlock a new class of halide electrolytes compatible with lithium metal anodes, enabling high-energy-density ASSBs.

### 2. In Situ TEM Study of Dynamic Interphase Evolution in ASSBs
- **Rationale:** The formation and evolution of interphases (e.g., CEI, SEI) are critical for ASSB performance but are poorly understood due to the lack of real-time, high-resolution characterization.
- **Feasibility:** Moderate. Requires specialized in situ TEM equipment and expertise. Sample preparation for solid-state cells is challenging but feasible.
- **Potential Impact:** Provide fundamental insights into interphase formation, guiding the rational design of stable interfaces for long-life ASSBs.

### 3. High-Throughput Computational Screening of High-Entropy MIECs for Cathodes
- **Rationale:** High-entropy mixed ionic and electronic conductors (MIECs) can simplify cathode design and improve performance, but the optimal composition is unknown.
- **Feasibility:** High. High-throughput DFT calculations can screen thousands of potential compositions for ionic/electronic conductivity and stability.
- **Potential Impact:** Accelerate the discovery of novel MIECs, leading to higher-power and longer-life ASSBs.

### 4. Multiscale Modeling of Mechanical Degradation in Sulfide-Based ASSBs
- **Rationale:** Sulfide electrolytes are soft and can accommodate volume changes, but the mechanical degradation of composite cathodes during cycling is a major concern.
- **Feasibility:** Moderate. Requires coupling of particle-scale mechanics (e.g., FEM) with cell-scale models. Experimental validation is needed.
- **Potential Impact:** Enable the design of mechanically robust composite cathodes, improving the cycle life of sulfide-based ASSBs.

### 5. AI-Driven Analysis of Operando Data for Solid-State Battery Diagnostics
- **Rationale:** Operando techniques (e.g., XRD, NMR, Raman) generate large, complex datasets that are difficult to interpret manually.
- **Feasibility:** High. Machine learning algorithms (e.g., neural networks, principal component analysis) can be trained to identify patterns and correlations in the data.
- **Potential Impact:** Develop advanced diagnostic tools for real-time monitoring and optimization of ASSB performance, accelerating research and development.
